policy work

Gender gaps in the labor market impact of the COVID-19 pandemic

with Claudia Hupkau

Gender gaps in the labor market impact of the COVID-19 pandemic are closely linked to the policies implemented to contain the spread of the virus, such as school and workplace closures, and the negative effects on the economy, such as financial support for short-time workers. In Spain and the UK, gender gaps in job loss were minimal, but women were more likely to enter short-time work or economic inactivity, especially those with children under 16. These gaps coincided with school closures and seemed to vanish once schools reopened. In contrast, the U.S. exhibited pronounced and persistent gender gaps, with women more likely to lose or leave jobs and become economically inactive, particularly mothers. These U.S. discrepancies are likely attributed to longer school closures and less generous employment protections. The findings suggest that the pandemic has exacerbated existing gender imbalances, particularly in domestic roles and childcare. To avoid undoing decades of progress in gender equality, policymakers should consider the gendered impacts of school closures, and work together with the private sector to ensure that new flexible work arrangements, such as increased teleworking, are adopted equally by men and women.
